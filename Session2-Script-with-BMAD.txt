--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

THE 5 THINGS AI NEEDS YOU TO PLAN
[Reveal these one by one as you discuss each. Build anticipation.]


1. A CLEAR PROBLEM STATEMENT

"If you start with 'Build me a cool AI tool,' the AI guesses. And it 
guesses wrong.

But if you start with: 'Customer service spends 3 hours a day answering 
repeat emails about account balances. Build something that answers those 
automatically.'

Now the AI understands the job to be done. The constraint. The success 
condition."


2. SMALL PIECES (MODULES)

"Remember the whiteboard? You can't dump 'the whole system' on it.

You plan small pieces: 'Email reader.' 'Topic detector.' 'Response 
generator.' 'Quality checker.'

Each piece fits on the whiteboard. The AI can handle it brilliantly. 
Then you connect them."


3. INPUTS & OUTPUTS (CONTRACTS)

"Every piece gets a little contract. 'Takes X, produces Y.'

This is how you stop prompts like 'Can you just make it better?' that 
lead to chaos.

Instead: 'The Topic Detector takes raw email text as input. It outputs 
one of five categories: Balance Inquiry, Transaction Dispute, Account 
Access, Card Issues, or Other.'

Clear. Testable. The AI can't misunderstand."


4. BUILD ORDER

"What comes first? What depends on what?

Build your safety check BEFORE the fancy features. Learn from my mistake."


5. VERIFICATION STEPS

"How will you know it worked? Not 'it looks good' but 'here's the test 
case that proves it.'"
