If anything drifted in Session 2…

Four things to put straight, from feedback after the last session.

"I need to get this onto an internal platform / into the backlog."

Not yet. Problem definition comes before solution deployment. Birmingham is where we explore — not where we ship.

"I have to solve a business problem end-to-end."

No. We're building AI capability and confidence. Showing what's possible. Sharpening how you articulate problems.

"What's the point of building externally if I can't use it internally?"

The understanding comes back inside. So does your sharper way of briefing AI. The prototype is evidence — for senior people, for proper investment.

"External tools look like a hurdle — I'm not sure I want to sign up."

Rip the plaster off. Register for one tool. The hesitation goes once you've used something for ten minutes. That confidence is half the point.
